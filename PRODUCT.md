# Product

## Register

product

## Users

- **Patients**: Individuals seeking healthcare services, booking appointments, managing their care journey. Use the platform on mobile (primary) and web.
- **Practitioners**: Healthcare providers (doctors, therapists, specialists) managing availability, consultations, and patient relationships. Use web dashboard primarily.
- **Admin Team**: Platform operators managing users, payments, content, and system configuration. Use web admin interface.

## Product Purpose

Fayed is a healthcare platform connecting patients with practitioners for booking and managing care sessions. The platform enables:
- Patient-practitioner matching and booking
- Session scheduling and management
- Secure payment processing
- Care chat between patients and practitioners
- Profile and journey tracking
- Practitioner availability and finance management

Success looks like: seamless booking experience, reliable session management, clear communication between all parties, and professional trust.

## Brand Personality

**Trustworthy · Professional · Calm**

The platform must feel credible and safe for healthcare decisions. Professional execution signals medical authority. Calm UX reduces anxiety around health concerns.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS aesthetic (over-styled startup look)
- Gamified or playful healthcare apps (not a game)
- Clinical/sterile feel (too cold, lacks warmth)
- Cluttered medical dashboards (too much data, overwhelming)

Reference: shezlong.com shows a clean, Arabic-first healthcare booking experience worth referencing.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over density** - Information must be scannable; healthcare decisions need clear options
2. **Trust through consistency** - Predictable patterns, reliable feedback, no surprises
3. **Accessibility as baseline** - Healthcare must work for everyone; Arabic/English support, reduced motion, screen reader friendly
4. **Calm interaction** - No aggressive CTAs, gentle transitions, patient pacing
5. **Professional confidence** - Clean typography, appropriate spacing, healthcare-appropriate color (blues, greens - not neon/flashy)

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum (AA for healthcare is standard)
- Full RTL support for Arabic interface
- Reduced motion support for accessibility
- Screen reader compatible (labels, roles, focus management)
- Color contrast meets 4.5:1 for text
- Touch targets minimum 44x44px on mobile
